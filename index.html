At every memorial, wedding, and milestone, a room fills with people who each hold a piece of someone. Then everyone goes home, and the pieces scatter.

A photo shows you the face. It doesn’t tell you the story behind the smile. Nanafy is a simple way to gather those stories while everyone is still in the room — before they leave with the crowd.

How it works

Three steps. No app for your guests to download.

You set it up once. Everyone else just points their phone at a code — the same thing they already do at a restaurant table.

1

Create a keepsake for your person or occasion

Add a name, a photo, and a few words. It takes a couple of minutes, and it’s yours — private, and only shared with the people you choose.

2

Share the QR code where people gather

Print it on the memorial card, set it on the wedding table, drop it into the invitation. One scan takes each guest straight to that page — not a generic homepage, but their page.

3

Everyone leaves a story — you keep them forever

Guests write a memory or record their voice, right there on their phone. Every story lands in one place, gathered into a keepsake you’ll still have long after the day is over.
